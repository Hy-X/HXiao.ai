Reliable AI systems must communicate not only what they predict, but how certain they are. In research contexts, calibration errors can be more damaging than obvious mistakes.

Uncertainty-aware reasoning helps teams distinguish between confident inference, plausible speculation, and unsupported generation.

Calibration as a First-Class Metric

Evaluation should track confidence alignment with correctness. A model that says "I don't know" appropriately is often more useful than one that answers every prompt with high confidence.

Implications for Scientific Work

In science and seismology pipelines, uncertainty signals guide where humans should review, rerun experiments, or collect additional data. This makes AI a better partner for rigorous research.
