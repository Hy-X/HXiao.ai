Reasoning Under Uncertainty: How LLMs Handle What They Don't Know

Calibration, confidence, and truthfulness in AI reasoning.

Hongyu Xiao
HXiao.ai · February 20, 2026 · 6 min read

Reliable AI systems must communicate not only what they predict, but how certain they are. In research contexts, calibration errors can be more damaging than obvious mistakes.

Uncertainty-aware reasoning helps teams distinguish between confident inference, plausible speculation, and unsupported output.

Calibration as a First-Class Metric

Evaluation should track confidence alignment with correctness. A model that says "I don't know" appropriately is often more useful than one that answers every prompt with high confidence.

Implications for Scientific Work

In science and seismology workflows, uncertainty signals indicate where humans should review results, rerun experiments, or collect additional data. This makes AI a stronger partner for rigorous research.
